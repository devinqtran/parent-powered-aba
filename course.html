Course Overview

Parent Powered ABA is designed to give parents the tools they need to implement effective ABA strategies at home. Our comprehensive curriculum covers everything from basic principles to advanced techniques that can be tailored to your child's unique needs.

Throughout this course, you'll learn how to:

  • Understand the principles behind Applied Behavior Analysis
  • Implement effective reinforcement strategies
  • Reduce challenging behaviors through positive approaches
  • Develop personalized skill-building programs
  • Track progress and make data-driven decisions

Whether you're new to ABA or looking to enhance your existing knowledge, this course provides practical strategies you can start using right away.

Course Units

Our curriculum is divided into 5 comprehensive units

Unit 1: ABA Foundations

Learn the core principles of Applied Behavior Analysis and how they can be applied in your home environment.

  • Understanding the science behind ABA
  • Identifying antecedents, behaviors, and consequences
  • Setting up your home environment for success
  • Basic data collection techniques
  • Establishing positive routines

Unit 2: Communication Development

Discover strategies to enhance your child's communication abilities, from basic requests to complex conversation skills.

  • Assessing current communication skills
  • Encouraging first words and requests
  • Building vocabulary through naturalistic teaching
  • Supporting alternative communication methods
  • Fostering back-and-forth interactions

Unit 3: Behavior Management

Learn positive approaches to address challenging behaviors and teach alternative, appropriate responses.

  • Understanding the function of behaviors
  • Preventing challenging behaviors
  • Implementing effective reinforcement strategies
  • Teaching self-regulation skills
  • Creating behavior intervention plans

Unit 4: Social Skills Development

Develop techniques to help your child build meaningful social connections and navigate social situations.

  • Teaching basic interaction skills
  • Building play skills with peers
  • Understanding and expressing emotions
  • Developing perspective-taking abilities
  • Navigating group settings and activities

Unit 5: Independence & Life Skills

Focus on developing the everyday skills your child needs to gain greater independence and confidence.

  • Task analysis for complex skills
  • Teaching self-help routines
  • Building adaptive and flexible thinking
  • Supporting transitions and changes
  • Preparing for school and community settings
